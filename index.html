Data Science

"In Gaia we trust. All others must bring data!" – so goes the adage (or thereabouts).
Though data alone will not an insight make. As a data scientist who has worked in both industry and academia, I know the value of data comes with contextual understanding and domain knowledge, with asking the right questions, and with appropriate tools to extract the information and insights needed. I put this ethos into effect in my academic research, as an analyst within the UKBiobank, and when meeting the needs of private clients.

Scientific Research

My research transects the fields of ecology, wildlife disease, conservation biology and evolution. In particular, my research aims to understand the dynamics of wild populations in the face of adverse events, such as disease outbreaks, pathogen infection, species reintroductions, and environmental change. I address these problems by using state-of-the-art modelling techniques, robust experimental designs and rigorous statistical analyses to build on current theory and improve management applications.

Data Analyst @ UK Biobank

UK Biobank is a large-scale biomedical database, containing health information for >500,000 participants. My role as an analyst at the UK Biobank is fast-paced and varied, ecompassing building data pipelines, providing statistical support to researchers and staff, automating tasks, data mining and modelling, and producing reports, dashboards, and data visualisations.

Customer insight analysis for the Curiosity Box

The Curiosity Box provides hands-on science and engineering activities to families and schools. They operate a subscription-based sicence activity box service with customers located throughout the UK and Europe. I conducted an in-depth analysis of their customer database, including demographic make-up, purchase history, site activity to provide key business insights to drive company decisions.

Recommendation System for the zooniverse.org

The Zooniverse is the world’s largest platform for people-powered research, where citizen scientists help professional researchers to conduct research that would not otherwise be possible or practical. To enhance user engagement and encourage Zooniverse users to participatee in a wider range of projects I was hired to build a recommendation system that would offer algorithm-based project suggestions to users based on their demonstrated interests and activity on the site.
